Case I: A Centralized Estimator with high error during cyber attacks

In this scenario we deploy the current state-of-art estimation aglorithms to dampen wide area oscillations in the power grid. The results of this scenario can be visulized in three panel below.

The panel on the left shows the geo-spatial distribution of synchophasors, as blue dots, in the western region of the nation. Some of the synchophasors are partiioned into four clusters. The estimation algorithm is deployed at the central location indicated by a star.

The right panel shows the network traffic in each cluster. The x-axis is time in seconds and the y-axis in volume of traffic in Mbits per second. In the absence of an attack, there is norminal data and control traffic on the network. The power grid is attacked in three stages. The first attack occurs at ~15 seconds, followed by two more at ~40 seconds and ~75 seconds each. These are indicated by an increase in the volume of network traffic on the right panel and as a red clusters in the left panel.

The lowermost panel plots the error in damping estimation. As seen in the plot below, during an attack the error spikes high which could result in cascading instabilities in the power grid.
