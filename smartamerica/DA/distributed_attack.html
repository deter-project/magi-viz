Case II: A Distributed Estimator resilient to cyber attacks

In this scenario we deploy a new distributed estimation algorithm to dampen oscillations in the power grid. The results of this scenario can be visualized in three panel below. The panel on the left shows the geo-spatial distribution of synchrophasors, as blue dots, in the western region of the nation. Some of the synchrophasors are partitioned into four clusters. A local estimator is deployed in each region and a global estimator is deployed to average the results as indicated by stars on the map.

The right panel shows the network traffic in each cluster. The x-axis is time in seconds and the y-axis in volume of traffic in Mbits per second. In the absence of an attack, there is norminal data and control traffic on the network. The power grid is attacked in three stages. The first attack occurs at ~12 seconds, followed by two more at ~48 seconds and ~85 seconds each. These are indicated by an increase in the volume of network traffic on the right panel and as a red clusters in the left panel.

The lowermost panel plots the error in damping estimation. As seen in the plot below, during an attack the error remains negligible ensuring that the power grid remains stable.
